HEIC vs JPG: which is better for iPhone photos?

Both formats are useful. HEIC saves storage and keeps strong quality, while JPG is still the most accepted format for sharing and uploads.

Why iPhone uses HEIC by default

  • HEIC files are usually smaller than JPG for similar quality.
  • Smaller files save space on your phone and iCloud.
  • Apple optimized iPhone cameras and workflows around HEIC.

When JPG is the better choice

  • When a website rejects HEIC uploads.
  • When a school or work form asks for JPG or PNG.
  • When sharing with someone on an older app or device.

Simple rule you can follow

Keep HEIC for normal iPhone use. Convert to JPG only when compatibility matters. It is the easiest format for “this won’t upload” situations.
